# Feedback Guide

OpsProbe is still in the early external-validation stage. The most useful feedback is concrete, workflow-based, and tied to a real inspection or reporting need.

## What To Submit

Use one of these feedback paths:

- `Inspection Need`
  When a check, template, service inspection, or evidence output is missing.
- `Report Feedback`
  When the report exists, but the audience fit, structure, wording, or density is wrong.
- `Workflow Friction`
  When OpsProbe slows you down, confuses you, or does not match the real sequence of work you need to complete.

## What Good Feedback Looks Like

High-signal feedback usually includes:

- the actual environment you inspect
- the task you were trying to finish
- what you do today without OpsProbe
- what was missing, confusing, or too expensive in time
- what output or workflow would have been more useful

## Feedback Prompts

If you are not sure where to start, answer these:

- What were you trying to inspect?
- Who needed the result?
- What action were they supposed to take after reading the report?
- What did OpsProbe not give you?
- What manual workaround did you fall back to?

## What To Avoid

Low-signal feedback is still welcome, but it is harder to act on. Examples:

- "Need more features"
- "The report is not good"
- "This is confusing"

Try to replace those with a real example, affected workflow, and the specific output you expected.
